Welcomer to (Not) Rubbish Robotics

We're a team of engineers from the University of Edinburgh: with a mission to build fully functioning robotic arms out of e-waste

Our goal is to inspire students in developing countries to harness e-waste to produce useful technology. We want to provide them with the knowledge and guidance needed to construct a robotic arm.

The Journey

We decided we wanted to build robots out of one type of e-waste. We decided to build the robot out of printers, given their ubiquity and reliable motors.

We got together and took apart 5 printers to figure out what kind of e-waste we could find in them. We found various motors (DC, Steppers), electromagnets, gears, belts, and a lot of screws.

We found a DC motor configuration that was present in 4 out of the 5 printers we dissasembled, and used this configuration to inform the design of our robotic arm

What we have achieved

We have designed a robot made completely out of e-waste: including an analogue controller using wires and buttons from the printer; capable of performing pick-and-place operations
