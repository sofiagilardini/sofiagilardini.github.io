Welcomer to (Not) Rubbish Robotics

(Not) Rubbish Robotics is a team of passionate engineers from The University of Edinburgh dedicated to reshaping the landscape of education and innovation. We're on a mission to harness the untapped potential of electronic waste (e-waste) and use it as a catalyst for transformative change in the field of STEM education. Our diverse team brings together skills in robotics, mechanical and electrical engineering to create a sustainable solution that empowers students and communities in Sub-Saharan Africa and beyond.

Our Mission

At (Not) Rubbish Robotics, our mission is clear: to revolutionize STEM education by repurposing discarded e-waste into functional robots. We're committed to tackling the challenges faced by students in developing countries, where access to quality education can be limited. Our goal is to make STEM education more accessible, hands-on, and engaging, ultimately contributing to improved social mobility and innovation.

Educational Package

Our educational package includes a parametric CAD model that allows users to customize their robot designs according to motor dimensions, a comprehensive manual that guides students through the entire disassembly and manufacturing journey, and open-source code for controlling the robot.

Head to our instructions page to find out how you can build your own!

You will find instructions on how to disassemble a printer, characterise the e-waste, manufacture your robot and control it buttons from your printer or an arduino and code!
