Our Mission

(Not) Rubbish Robotics is a team of passionate engineers from The University of Edinburgh dedicated to creating practical Engineering learning opportunities for University students in Sub-Saharan Africa.

We're on a mission to harness the untapped potential of electronic waste (e-waste) and use it as a catalyst for transformative change in the field of STEM education. Our diverse team brings together skills in robotics, mechanical and electrical engineering to create a sustainable solution that empowers students and communities in Sub-Saharan Africa and beyond.

Specifically, we sought to build a robot completely out of e-waste, to prove that it is possible and inspire students to do the same.

We chose to build a robot arm because it is a versatile tool that can be used to teach a wide range of subjects, including mechanics, electronics, and programming. We also wanted to build a robot arm because it is a tool that is used in industry, and it will give students the opportunity to learn skills that would be useful in the workplace.
